When I meet people they are often surprised to learn that my background is Chemistry. They ask me what I did during the time I did research in Chemistry and I will explain briefly what I did.

Since childhood I have been fascinated with Chemistry (more specifically with bread fabrication process. When I was a child I dreamed of being a baker :P) and during the BSc and MSc degrees, my main goal was to follow an academic career studying smart materials and biomaterials. With that in mind, I spent about three years researching in the organic chemistry field.

Me when I was 20 years old with Professor Peter Atkins - Best Poster Award @ 3rd ENEQUI conference.

There, I worked with ionic liquids in two distinct directions, the first as drug encapsulation agents. The second and most crazy, as element in the development of a new electrolyte polymer. In all of this work it was used NMR spectroscopy technique, mainly in CENIMAT NMR laboratory @ FCT NOVA.

NMR spectrometer used to gel’s study in my MSc thesis.

During my MSc degree in Bioorganic Chemistry @ FCT NOVA I’ve developed and fully investigated a new cellulose polymeric material. The project was extremely interesting since the use of cellulose as a backbone of polymer ionic liquids allows the production of new materials that bring together some of the unique properties of ionic liquids with the intrinsic polymer features. Since the material has shown promising results, a patent was submitted.

In this project I felt the need to study NMR T1 / T2 relaxation times of gel molecules. The mathematical method is a bit complex because the material is a gel, and that is when I first used the python skills I had at the time.

After finish my MSc degree I decided to improve my knowledge and enrolled on Andrew Ng’s Machine Learning course (indeed, this is an amazing introdutory course for Machine Learning!), Smart Ninja programming weekend and Lisbon meetups. (In the future I will share with you books, blogs, online courses, Lisbon's meetups and the people who helped me on this journey).

After spending a year and a half in a consulting company, I felt the need to start document all the work I have done. Consequently, I build up a GitHub repository with Python's exercises and Kaggle’s challenges. Because of that I have being spending much more time practicing / solving challenges than reading books on machine learning. More recently I have been strengthen the knowledge already acquired and set goals from now on.

My first big Kaggle’s challenge was Flights Delays and Cancellations, where the goal is to predict when a flight is delayed. In that challenge I went through all data science steps: basic statistics, build a baseline, data exploration / understanding, feature engineering and model iteration. I recommend this challenge because it is a medium complex problem where you can practice feature engineering, the dataset is not that big, work with a temporal component and others.

My Data Science GitHub repository.
